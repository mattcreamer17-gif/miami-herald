A read on where AI fits in the Miami Herald's stack: and where it doesn't.

By Matt Creamer, CRO, Eduba. Dateline: Miami.

Opening

Two weeks ago TheWrap named the Herald as one of three McClatchy papers where the content scaling agent is live. The CWA Local 3108 contract already spells out byline control, transcription, summarization, and disclosure language. That is further down the governance road than most newsrooms in the country. It is also the point where the governance frame stops being paperwork and starts being an engineering problem.

The frame

Most AI rollouts in newsrooms are priced as if everything is an AI problem. Most of it is not. A practical breakdown looks closer to 60 percent traditional code and database work (byline provenance, review queues, translation memory, paywall logic), 30 percent rule-based logic (disclosure rules, publishing policy, reviewer assignment), and 10 percent actual generative AI (drafting, summarization, image tagging). The tool stack you already have (Elvex, Nota, the scaling agent, Hurricane Bot) is reasonable. The part underneath it, the orchestration layer that says which problem belongs on which layer, is where the trust work gets done.

The orchestration layer is where the trust work gets done.

The case

KPMG UK, one of the Big Four. Forty-plus executives trained on AI orchestration for a regulated-industry environment where credibility and attribution are the product. The delivery pattern was a leadership workshop, a governance frame, and case work against their own material. Newsrooms and professional services firms sit in the same category of buyer. The thing on the line is public trust, and the people who sign off on it have to be able to defend their decisions in public.

The paper

Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM) treats an agent's context as a layered filesystem that a human can read and audit. Published: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.

For governance-minded readers, the companion work is the Ethics Engine, a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. Preprint: arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742. Repository: github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine.
